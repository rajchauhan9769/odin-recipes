Rasmalai

Description

Rasmalai is a traditional Indian dessert made of soft, spongy cheese dumplings soaked in a rich, sweetened milk flavored with cardamom and saffron. It is often garnished with chopped almonds and pistachios, giving it a delicate aroma and a creamy texture.

Known for its light, refreshing taste and melt-in-your-mouth consistency, Rasmalai is a favorite dessert served during festivals, weddings, and other special occasions across India.

Ingredients

  • 1 litre full-fat milk
  • 250 g paneer (fresh or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 1 litre water
  • 200 g sugar (for sugar syrup)
  • 500 ml full-fat milk (for rabri)
  • 3 tablespoons sugar (for rabri)
  • ¼ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped pistachios
  • 1 tablespoon sliced almonds
  • ½ teaspoon rose water (optional)

Steps

  1. Crumble the paneer and knead it with the cornflour until it becomes smooth and soft.
  2. Divide the dough into 10-12 equal portions and roll each into a smooth ball. Flatten them slightly into small discs.
  3. In a large pan, bring the water and 200 g sugar to a boil to make the sugar syrup.
  4. Carefully add the paneer discs to the boiling syrup. Cover and cook on medium-high heat for 12-15 minutes until they double in size.
  5. Remove the discs and let them cool slightly. Gently squeeze out the excess syrup.
  6. In another saucepan, bring the 500 ml milk to a gentle boil. Add the 3 tablespoons sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron. Simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the milk thickens slightly.
  7. Remove the milk from the heat and stir in the rose water, if using.
  8. Place the cooked paneer discs into the warm milk mixture and let them soak for at least 1 hour.
  9. Garnish with chopped pistachios and sliced almonds.
  10. Refrigerate for a few hours and serve chilled.
